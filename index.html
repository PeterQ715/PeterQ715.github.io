Images of the Russian Empire: Colorizing the Prokudin-Gorskii photo collection

Sergei Mikhailovich Prokudin-Gorskii (1863-1944) was a visionary photographer who pioneered color photography in early 1900s Russia. With the Tsar's permission, he traveled across the Russian Empire taking thousands of color photographs using red, green, and blue filtered exposures on glass plates. This project implements image alignment algorithms to automatically reconstruct color images from Prokudin-Gorskii's digitized glass plate negatives, using single-scale and multiscale pyramid approaches to handle both low and high-resolution images effectively.
